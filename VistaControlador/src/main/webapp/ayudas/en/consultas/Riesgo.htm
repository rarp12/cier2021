Objective

This query contains the information on natural and anthropic risks.

This query window allows the user to visualize each one of the risks associated to the institutional land along with its corresponding conditions in order to define its level of critical nature, keeping in mind the following definition:

Critical, this will be education premises with at least one risk under unfavorable conditions, meaning one (1), in all other cases it will be Non Critical.  

For instance: if land A has the following risk information: 

RISKS

CONDITION

Landslides

1

Frosts

4

This institutional land classifies as critical

For the aggregation levels of establishments, departments etc., it identifies the number of lands which present risks and the proportion of lands according to each condition, which must be selected in the filter. It also shows the affected population including students, teachers and administrative staff. 

 

Consulting Natural risks

To make a query by Generalities, you need to click on scope query and select risks-Natural risks on the drop-down list.

Once youve entered, you will be able to see the following screen:  

Natural Risks Features

·         Risk type: this variable allows us to analyze the existence of the following types of natural risks: landslides,  Volcanic Eruption, Frosts, Hurricane, Forest Fire, Floods, others, Droughts, seismic, tsunamis   

 

·         Risk Condition: this variable defines the level of probability of occurrence of a particular risk on a scale from 1 to 4, in reference to the following definitions:

4-There is risk affectation with total mitigation.              
3 There is partial risk affectation with partial mitigation
2- There is partial risk affectation with no mitigation whatsoever.         
1- There is absolute risk affectation with no mitigation whatsoever.

 

Consulting anthropic risks

To make a query by Generalities, you need to click on scope query and select risks-Anthropic risks on the drop-down list.

Once youve entered, you will be able to see the following screen:  

 

Anthropic Risks Features

·         Type of Risk: this variable allows us to analyze the existence of the following anthropic risks: storage. Chemicals/Combustibles, freeway, Noise Pollution, Air Pollution, structurally vulnerable buildings, electrical wiring/antennas, Mining, Public Order, Others, Landfills/ cemeteries. 

 

·         Risk Condition:  this variable defines the level of probability of occurrence of a particular risk on a scale from 1 to 4, in reference to the following definitions:

4-it is located farther than the minimum distance from the identified risk. (Regulation)
3- it is located at the minimum distance (Regulation) from the identified risk.
2-it is located below the minimum distance (Regulation)   from the identified risk (partial affectations).   
1- It is located below the minimum distance (Regulation)   from the identified risk (total affectations).

·         Type of Separation: this area shows the types of separation which exist between the institutional lands and the elements of their surroundings, in reference to: water bodies, Air approach path, Railway, others, underground networks, Natural Reserve, Rivers or Canals, Road.
