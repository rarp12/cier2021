Objectives

This window provides information concerning the characteristics on the condition, quality and use of the constructions.

 

Buildings

When querying buildings you will see information on the type and condition of the following materials: Front of building, finishes of facade, Roof.

Similarly, the conditions of the materials of each of the buildings and the institutional land can be analyzed.   

 

Features of the Building

·         Year of construction: This variable allows the user to establish a range of dates to see the institutional lands which comply with certain conditions.

 

·         Facade Wall Material: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in the educational lands for the facades, in reference to: Solid adobe,  Bahareque(mud), Drywall, Coated metal structure, metal structure in sight, reinforced concrete/ Concrete, brick/Block, Wood, Mesh, Others, PVC azembla type, Superboard, without wall, adobe partition walls, Zinc

 

·         Finishes of Facade Material: This allows us to identify the prevailing material used in the educational lands for the finishes of the facade, in reference to: Wall Material, Others, wall material plastering, Wall Material Paint, plastering Paint.

 

·         Roof Material: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in the educational lands for the roof, in reference to: Alusin, Aluzinc, Cane and mud/clay, Vegetal Material Roof, Concrete, Duralita or conduit, Mat, enameled sheet, Brass Sheet, Zinc sheet/Galvanized/Corrugated iron, Others, Palm/hay, without roof, asbestos cement roof tile, Mud or clay roof tile, Fiber cement tile, Metal sheet roof tile, Thermo-acoustic tile.

 

·         Material Condition This variable lets us know the condition of the materials in institutional lands, according to a quantitative scale from one (1) to four (4), keeping in mind the following:

4- its in normal condition and shows periodic maintenance.

3- It shows minor deterioration and requires precautionary maintenance.

2- It presents damage or considerable partial installations which require maintenance work, (may cause accidents)

1- It presents considerable damage as a whole which require maintenance work and change of elements, (may cause accidents)

 

·         Condition Material of Building: This variable classifies buildings according to the condition of its materials, in reference to:   

Critical
Noncritical

It must be kept in mind that whenever 50% of a buildings materials are in condition one (1) or two (2), it will be classified as critical, in all other cases its noncritical.  

·         Condition Material of land: This variable classifies lands according to the number of building square meters in critical condition, in reference to:

Critical
Noncritical

It must be kept in mind that whenever 50% or more of a lands buildings square meters are in critical condition, it will be classified as critical, in all other cases its considered noncritical

 

Spaces

When querying spaces you will see information on the type and condition of the following materials: walls, Wall finishes, Doors, Windows, Ceilings, and Floor

Similarly itll generate the number of square meters for each type according to the material and its condition and it can quantify the number of spaces as well.  

Features of Spaces

·         Year of Construction: This variable allows the user to establish a range of dates to see the institutional lands which comply with certain conditions.

 

·         Material of Wall: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in each building of  educational lands for walls, in reference to: Solid adobe,  Bahareque(mud), Drywall, Coated metal structure, visible metal structure, reinforced concrete/ Concrete, brick/Block, Wood, Mesh, Others, PVC azembla type, Superboard, without wall, adobe partition walls, Zinc

 

·         Material Wall finishes: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in each building of  educational lands for Wall finishes, in reference to: finishes material of wall itself,  Ceramic tile, Others, Plastering on wall material, Frieze paper, Paint on stucco , Paint on wall material, Paint on Plastering, just stucco

 

·         Door Material: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in each building of educational lands for doors, in reference to: Wood, Metal, Others, without door, Glass.

 

·         Windows Material: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in each building of educational lands for windows, in reference to: Aluminum blind, Lattice, Metal and Glass Frame, Metal and Wooden frame, Aluminum and Glass Frame, Metal Blind, Others, window without frame, Aluminum only, Wood only, Metal only, gate only or iron bars.

 

·         Floor Material: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in each building of educational lands for floors, in reference to: Cobblestone, Asphalt sand, Ceramic tiles, cement tiles, Concrete/ liquid cement, Granite, Synthetic Turf, Wood, Others , Stone, Synthetic floor, No floor, Sand, Green Area/Grass

 

·         Ceiling Material: This variable allows us to identify the prevailing material used in each building of educational lands for ceilings, in reference to:  Drywall/Plaster, Fiber cement, Styrofoam, Wood, Metal, Others, Plastering, Panelit, No ceiling

 

·         Condition of material : This variable allows us to know the condition of the materials for each of the institutional lands, according to a quantitative scale from one (1) to four (4), keeping in mind the following:

4 its in normal condition and shows periodic maintenance.

3 It shows minor deterioration and requires precautionary maintenance.

 2 It presents damage or considerable partial installations which require maintenance work, (may cause accidents)

1 It presents considerable damage as a whole which require maintenance work and change of elements, (may cause accidents)
